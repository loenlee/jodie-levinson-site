My approach

Talk therapy, plus a channel for what talk can't reach

I've spent the last seven years working with individuals and families navigating anxiety, depression, relationship challenges, and major life transitions. My training combines verbal processing with music and creative arts, so we're never limited to only the feelings you can name out loud.

Sessions are collaborative and paced to you — some weeks that looks like conversation, others it looks like sound, rhythm, or creative expression doing the work words haven't gotten to yet. The goal throughout is the same: more emotional regulation, more insight, and a clearer, steadier relationship with yourself.

I earned my Master's in Music Psychotherapy from New York University and I'm a Licensed Creative Arts Therapist and Board-Certified Music Therapist in New York State.

Good questions

FAQ

Do I need to be musical for music therapy to work?

No. No musical background or skill is required — music is used as a tool for processing and expression, not performance.

Is this different from traditional talk therapy?

We use talk therapy as a foundation and add music and creative process where it's useful — you're never required to use them if conversation is what serves you best.
